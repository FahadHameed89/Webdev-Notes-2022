Terry Pratchett

Warmly regarded as 'the guy who wrote Discworld', Terence David John Pratchett (28 April 1948 - 12 March 2015) was a british fantasy author most famous for his 41 book series known as Discworld.

Here's a Bibliography of Terry's Discworld series:

  • April 1948 - Terry Pratchett is born
  • 1971 - First published novel, The Carpet People
  • 1983 - Published 'The Colour of Magic' (Discworld #1)
  • 1998 - Appointed Officer of the Order of the British Empire (OBE)
  • 2009 - Knighted for his services to literature
  • 2001 - Won the Carnegie Medal for 'The Amazing Maruce and his Educated Rodents', the first Discworld book marketed for children
  • 2007 - Announced that he had been diagnosed with early-onset Alzheimer's disease
  • 2008 Worked with the BBC to make a two-part documentary series about his Illness, TerryPratchett: Living with Alzheimer's.
  • 2010 - Recieved the World Fantasy Award for Life Achievement
  • March 2015 - Died, age 66.
  • August 2015 - Published 'The Shepard's Crown (Discworld #41)
